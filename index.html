PETMATCH
DESIGNING A WEBSITE TO HELP PRE-PET OWNERS TO FIND
THEIR IDEAL PET


Our team chose to focus on developing a responsive website for people looking to adopt, foster, or purchase pets. Seeking to understand the current problems that users face during the pet searching experience, we proceeded by conducting pop-up research.

We wanted to learn the specifics about the entitre pet searching process, hence a directed storytelling technique was the most appropriate.
The initial user base we identified were people who had gotten any pet, either pre- or post-Covid.
To launch the story, our first question was: could you tell me about a specific time when you adopted, fostered, or purchased a pet?

With the fourth and final project drawing to a close, it’s amazing to see how we’ve applied earlier concepts we’ve learned in class to this project. From grid hierarchy to
microinteractions, these concepts help strengthen the value of our design. What we take away from this course doesn’t end here as we will continue to build on these core concepts and apply them in future projects.
